Biography

I’m a third-year Bachelor of Arts student in Information Technology at York University, with a focused interest in Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) and data-driven decision-making. I’m passionate about aligning technology with business goals to help organizations build secure, efficient, and compliant systems.

My academic and project work has centered around cybersecurity, IT auditing, and analytics. I’m currently building my technical skills in Python, Java, and data interpretation, and I enjoy exploring how data can be used to assess risk, inform compliance strategies, and support innovation. I’ve had hands-on experience working with system architecture, smart technologies, and usability testing, which has given me a solid foundation in both back-end logic and front-end design thinking.

I’m especially interested in roles that involve evaluating risk, improving IT governance, and leveraging insights to support smarter decision-making. Whether it’s conducting an audit, analyzing patterns in system performance, or enhancing user experience, I thrive when I’m contributing to solutions that balance technical precision with real-world impact.

Outside of academics, I volunteer with hEr VOLUTION, where I help support programs that encourage young women to explore careers in STEM. I’m also an avid cozy gamer. Whether I’m playing my Nintendo Switch or organizing my collection of blind boxes, I enjoy relaxing with creative and calming games. These hobbies not only bring me joy but also reflect my love of detail, curiosity, and continuous exploration.

I’m currently open to co-op, internship, and mentorship opportunities that support my growth in GRC, IT auditing, and data analytics. I’m eager to contribute to meaningful work while learning from professionals in the field.
